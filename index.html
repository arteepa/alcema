Our History

In 1920 our farm was established in Huatabampo, Sonora, Mexico. When a Spanish emigrant, Eugenio Larrinaga found perfect land for vegetable farming.

His son Cesar Larrinaga continue farming the same land years after and began the remarkable trajectory that now has made him a recognized entrepreneur and pioneer of the Mayo valley, creating thousands of employments along the way and always keeping as a priority to take care of our planet.

With his innovative vision, work and commitment, he founded ALCEMA with his three sons Aldo, Cesar and Mario. Together, they have been able to develop the company to a level where our products end at the counters of the most important retailers in the US and leaving lots of work ahead to the next generation, that now are contributing in the company’s growth.

Why organic?

Practicing regenerative organic agriculture is helping the planet to cool down and heal, creating higher-quality food, that is far more nutritious and delicious than anything our worn-out industrial farms can produce, at the same time.

It includes any agricultural practice that increases soil organic matter from baseline levels and over time, provides long-term economic stability for farmers and ranchers, and creates resilient ecosystems and communities.

We can produce what we need and revitalize soil at the same time, thereby sequestering carbon currently polluting the atmosphere and warming our planet.

We have choose to find the way to get along with our fragile planet. Creating innovative production techniques; in which we understand that agriculture, like any other system has limits. it’s natural resources cannot be unconsciously exploded, and we have been working to contribute, by resolving to completely transform our operation into a sustainable system. Being one of the firsts companies in México to be organic certified and fulfilling the duty of leaving a better planet to next generations than what we got.

We believe in regenerative organic agriculture. The use of this term reflects firm commitments from farmers that ensure they don’t use GMO seeds or synthetic pesticides, period. In addition, they must take a holistic and locally tailored approach to land management in order to rebuild soil ecosystems.

Organic was just one step in a long journey to protect the planet and human health through agriculture.
